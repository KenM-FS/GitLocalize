                                                                    The interesting thing about all of this is that you have no statistical information about detection, deterrence and prevention, to show effectiveness measurements for the various techniques you use.                                                                                                                                                   Mandros Børge Nøst • 3 years ago • edited

                                                                                                                                                  TLDR: the best anticheats options are also the worst options for stable and responsive game experiences.

                                                                                                                                                  Client side simulation is essential in most situations if you want a fluent experience. Your post is heavily underestimating the networking implications in terms of latency and server-side processing if the simulation is only done in the server with no extrapolation on client side and an authoritative state needs to be sent in a timely manner to hundreds of users at once. You would need higher packet rates in order for the game to be responsive and you would also need network connections with no jitter at all. Even if you have that, you would have to dedicate huge amounts of server resources to correctly process information from lots of users simultaneously.

                                                                                                                                                  With no inside knowledge, WoW originally grew a lot in not so much time, meaning that they could not afford high cost servers and back then the users didn't have good internet connections compared to now. Having that constraint would reduce the potential community of users to those that had good internet connections.

                                                                                                                                                  After that point in time you could argue that the user population dropped and the average internet connections got better, but I guess that "technical debt" on the client side meant that changing its behavior so drastically was impossible.

                                                                                                                                                  Once Blizz decided to further split users in servers (sharding or phasing)... That meant they could either go for a high resourced option that improved the game and made cheats more difficult or just adapt resources to demand. The greedy option to remain profitable even if they had a third of the all time peak users is the latter.
